Wiener Schnitzel

Now we're talking. I'd not had home made Schnitzel prior to making this and it's definitely the 'schnit' (ugh..I know) I didn't have veal but went with Chicken, but this was just amazing with a squeeze of lemon and some coleslaw.

Ingredients

Note - measurements on these are vague - you'll need enough egg/flour/breadcrumbs to coat the chicken but I've not managed to figure out how much that is. Buy plenty, and then whatever you don't use can be used next time!

  • Chicken Cutlets
  • Egg
  • Flour
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Oil (Any type)
  • Salt
  • Lemon for garnish
Instructions
  1. Place chicken cutlets on a hard surface and cover with cling film. Smash it with a meat hammer, needs to be under 1cm thickness - slice breasts in 2 if easier!
  2. Beat the eggs, then make sure the flour, eggs and breadcrumbs are in bowls ready to dip the cutlets!
  3. Dip the cutlets in Flour > Egg > Breadcrumbs in that order.
  4. Heat lots of oil (at least 1/4 inch) in a large pan until it’s starting to smoke.
  5. Cook the shnitzel for 3 mins on one side. Make sure they don’t stick to the pan
  6. Turn and cook for 3 mins on the other side. Both sides need to be golden brown, internal meat temp of 63 degrees
Verdict

These are maybe the perfect fast food treat, they're so good. 9/10
